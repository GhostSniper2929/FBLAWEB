THE SOLAR SYSTEM

Explore the eight planets that orbit our Sun. Learn about their unique characteristics, atmospheres, and place in our cosmic neighborhood.

MERCURY

The smallest planet and closest to the Sun. Surface temperatures range from -290°F to 800°F.

Distance from Sun: 36 million miles

Day Length: 59 Earth days

Year Length: 88 Earth days

Moons: 0

VENUS

The hottest planet with a thick, toxic atmosphere. Known as Earth's twin due to similar size.

Distance from Sun: 67 million miles

Day Length: 243 Earth days

Year Length: 225 Earth days

Moons: 0

EARTH

Our home planet. The only known world with life. Has liquid water and a protective atmosphere.

Distance from Sun: 93 million miles

Day Length: 24 hours

Year Length: 365.25 days

Moons: 1 (The Moon)

MARS

The Red Planet. Has the largest volcano in the solar system and evidence of ancient water.

Distance from Sun: 142 million miles

Day Length: 24.6 hours

Year Length: 687 Earth days

Moons: 2 (Phobos, Deimos)

JUPITER

The largest planet. A gas giant with a Great Red Spot storm and over 90 moons.

Distance from Sun: 484 million miles

Day Length: 10 hours

Year Length: 12 Earth years

Moons: 95+ (Io, Europa, Ganymede, Callisto)

SATURN

Known for its spectacular rings made of ice and rock. Less dense than water - it would float!

Distance from Sun: 886 million miles

Day Length: 10.7 hours

Year Length: 29 Earth years

Moons: 146+ (Titan, Enceladus)

URANUS

An ice giant that rotates on its side. Has faint rings and a methane-rich atmosphere.

Distance from Sun: 1.8 billion miles

Day Length: 17 hours

Year Length: 84 Earth years

Moons: 27 (Miranda, Titania, Oberon)

NEPTUNE

The windiest planet with speeds up to 1,200 mph. Deep blue color from methane in atmosphere.

Distance from Sun: 2.8 billion miles

Day Length: 16 hours

Year Length: 165 Earth years

Moons: 16 (Triton)

PLANET CATEGORIES

TERRESTRIAL PLANETS

Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars are rocky planets with solid surfaces. They have similar internal structures with a core, mantle, and crust.

  • Dense, rocky composition
  • Solid surfaces
  • Thinner atmospheres (except Venus)
  • Fewer or no moons

GAS GIANTS & ICE GIANTS

Jupiter and Saturn are gas giants, while Uranus and Neptune are ice giants. They have no solid surfaces and are much larger than terrestrial planets.

  • No solid surface
  • Thick atmospheres
  • Many moons and ring systems
  • Extreme pressures and temperatures
